# Forge Novel — LitRPG Genre Conventions & Reader Contract

*The third reference layer. **REFERENCE.md** is the **what** (locked canon).
**WRITING_RULES.md** is the **how** (voice, prose, theology-in-craft). This is
the **why it lands** — the genre contract the LitRPG reader brings to the page,
and exactly where forge-novel honors it, bends it, or breaks it on purpose.*

*Distilled from David's 17-book style bank (see
`reference_litrpg_style_sources.md` / `Book Samples/`) — patterns and
reader-expectations only, no source text reproduced.*

---

## Authority — this layer yields

This doc is a **reference, not a rule.** When it conflicts with anything above it,
the higher layer wins, in this order:

1. David's in-session direction
2. `WRITING_RULES.md` (craft) and `REFERENCE.md` (canon)
3. The audiobook master tiebreaker (audio-flow beats page-sophistication)
4. **This doc** (reader-expectation contract)

A genre convention never overrides canon, craft, or the audiobook. It tells you
what the reader *expects*, so that when forge-novel diverges, the divergence is a
**choice** and not an accident.

---

## How to use this doc

Every convention below carries a **stance**:

- **HONOR** — meet the expectation straight. The reader gets what they came for.
- **BEND** — deliver the underlying *payoff* by forge-novel's own means. The
  reader's *need* is met; the *form* differs.
- **BREAK** — deliberately refuse the expectation. A break must be intentional
  and earn its place. **An accidental break is a bug; a deliberate one is the
  book's signature.**

The check, every time a beat leans on a convention: *are we honoring, bending,
or breaking it — and is the stance the one this doc records?* If the prose drifts
into a stance this doc does **not** record (a spreadsheet stat-dump, a mid-action
level-up ding, the System reading as neutral tech), that is a finding.

## 1. The System & Interface

| Convention | Reader expects | Stance | How forge-novel does it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Status boxes / blue screens | Floating blue panels everyone shares | **BEND** | Nate's interface is a **fighter-pilot HUD** (Codex #95 Dual Interface), private per-user. Panels exist but render as his wireframe cockpit and route through Flint / the Storyteller — **show effects, not UI** (POV discipline). |
| Stat sheets / character pages | Full attribute screens, point allocation | **BEND** | Crunch guardrails: stat blocks **≤6 lines**, never full-page sheets, numbers shown *in play* not audited. The anti-Primal-Hunter dial; toward Cradle's near-zero interface. |
| Level-up "ding" + notification spam | Constant pings, XP bars, kill-confirms | **BEND** | Level-ups land at **scene transitions / chapter ends, never mid-action**. No dopamine feed. Flint narrates the number-work with personality. |
| Tooltips / auditable skill text | Game-accurate ability text to track | **BEND** | **Numbers are atmosphere, not audit-grade** — don't run a tally. Flint gives fuzzy word-descriptions early ("about ninety percent"); precise readouts arrive only as his translator arc earns them. |

## 2. Progression & Payoff (the dopamine contract)

| Convention | Reader expects | Stance | How forge-novel does it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Numbers-go-up dopamine | The visible hit of earned growth | **HONOR payoff / BEND delivery** | The *satisfaction* of earned growth is sacred; the *mechanism* is Flint's voice + felt consequence, not a stat feed. Contract met: growth stays visible and earned. |
| Build optimization / theorycrafting | Loadout scenes, skill-tree agonizing | **BREAK** | No loadout-optimization scenes. Nate picks what fits, decided same scene. Spreadsheet-paragraph deliberation is an explicit anti-pattern. |
| OP-but-earned power curve | MC gets strong; power is earned, not gifted | **HONOR** | Conduit power is earned through alignment, costs embodied weight, and is governed by *alignment, not permission*. Dresden-model earned escalation with permanent stakes. |
| Milestone / class-up reveals | Big satisfying unlocks at thresholds | **HONOR** | Tutorial graduation, class ID, specialization land as genuine milestone reveals — tied to **choice and character**, not pure mechanical gates. |

## 3. Tutorial / Onboarding Logic

| Convention | Reader expects | Stance | How forge-novel does it |
|---|---|---|---|
| The tutorial zone | A bounded newbie zone that teaches the rules alongside the reader | **HONOR** | 6-floor / ~2-week Tutorial; the reader learns the System as the cohort does. Classic genre onboarding — played straight. |
| Safe rooms / rest areas | Breather rooms between escalating fights | **HONOR + twist** | Safe Rooms between floors, with the **name-tag grace** twist (each rest strips one field — Name → Class → Rank). |
| The cohort / starting party | A band of fellow newbies; some die | **HONOR** | 12-survivor cohort with a canon casualty (Tyler, Ch6). Seeds the found-family. |
| First class assignment | The MC gets a class — often a "weird/useless" one | **HONOR + invert** | Nate reads as Anomaly / no-clean-class (the genre's broken-talent beat) — but the inversion is **hidden, not flashy** (see §4). |

## 4. Power, Stakes & the Underdog

| Convention | Reader expects | Stance | How forge-novel does it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Inverted "useless" talent, secretly OP | The detrimental-looking class is broken-strong (*Path of Ascension*, *World of Magic*, *He Who Fights With Monsters*) | **HONOR trope / INVERT its visibility** | Nate's Anomaly/Conduit reads as **less** on the HUD (fewer stats, `[PROCESSING]` tags) while his real effect is **more**. The genre shows the hidden OP as flash; forge-novel hides it as understatement. The "secretly broken" pleasure is preserved — delivered quietly, and he learns to hide it. |
| Competence porn | The joy of watching a skilled MC solve problems | **HONOR** | Nate's maker's eye / stockman's sense / engineer lens is grounded in real trade knowledge — competence-pleasure with ranch-and-shop authenticity. |
| Permanent stakes / death has weight | Death matters; consequences stick | **HONOR** | The 35-lives / vessel / corpse-run / Cornerstone-respawn mechanic gives death real cost without cheapening it. Tutorial death = ejection, not erasure. |
| System-Apocalypse / Integration premise | The world breaks; survivors adapt to new rules | **HONOR** | Integration / Veil-break is the System-Apocalypse subgenre done straight (*Obelisk*, *Defiance of the Fall*, *System Universe* lineage). |

## 5. Cast & Social Furniture

| Convention | Reader expects | Stance | How forge-novel does it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Snarky AI companion | A wisecracking guide-in-your-head (Eve in *Forge Master*, etc.) | **HONOR + secret** | Flint *is* the genre's AI companion — with the Spirit-rewrite / quantum-substrate truth hidden under the hood (reader doesn't know yet; scheduled reveal). |
| The loot goblin / scavenger | The party member who hoards every drop | **HONOR** | Josie literally *is* "The Loot Goblin" (Codex #102) — played straight, primary comic relief alongside Flint. |
| Mentor / tactical lead | An older head who reads people and runs the group | **HONOR** | Marcus (Vanguard) reads people while Nate reads architecture — complementary command. |
| Found family through shared trauma | The party becomes a family | **HONOR** | Core series theme (Dresden / *Genesis Online* lineage). |
| Animal companion / bonded beast | A pet that fights and bonds | **HONOR + deepen** | Rex/Judge pack-bond is the bonded-beast staple (Atticus + Oberon precedent), deepened into a relational-faculty mechanic and **contrasted against the System's Beast Master counterfeit**. |

## 6. Pacing & Serialization

| Convention | Reader expects | Stance | How forge-novel does it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Short chapters, constant momentum | Quick chapters, always a hook | **HONOR** | Dresden / Royal Road cadence; every chapter carries forward-momentum — no passive, stakes-free sequences. |
| Chapter-end hooks | Each chapter ends pulling you onward | **HONOR (with variety)** | Honored — but **not the same structural ending two chapters running** (the editor checks this). |
| Escalation ladder | Bigger threats each arc; the climb tops itself | **HONOR** | Floor-by-floor Tutorial escalation → custom Briarknight boss. |
| Self-contained arcs in a long climb | Each book a complete arc inside the 7-book series | **HONOR** | A complete arc per book across the 7-book climb. |

## 7. Genre Furniture

| Convention | Reader expects | Stance | How forge-novel does it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classes / skills / abilities | A named class, a skill list, leveled abilities | **HONOR (taxonomy'd)** | Domain → **Skill** (rank container) → **Ability** (named move) hierarchy, Codex #177. |
| Rarity tiers / item grades | Common → Legendary color tiers | **HONOR (lightly)** | Present but not fetishized — loot serves the story, not a spreadsheet. |
| Monster scaling / boss tiers | Mobs scale; bosses are set-pieces | **HONOR** | Thornling → Thornguard → Briarknight scaling; the boss as a custom, learned set-piece. |
| Crafting | Make/upgrade gear; the crafter-MC pleasure | **HONOR** | Nate's Engineer / maker's eye — crafter-combat hybrid (*Forge Master* lineage); even-weighted on combat **and** crafting. |
| Secular power-fantasy frame | Power for its own sake; no metaphysical spine | **BREAK** | Unified-source theology + System-as-counterfeit is the book's spine — shown **Narnia-model, never preached.** The single biggest deliberate divergence. |

## forge-novel's Signature Divergences (the deliberate breaks — don't re-litigate)

A gathered quick-list so these intentional breaks are never mistaken for bugs on
an editorial pass:

1. **Theological spine** *(BREAK the secular default)* — unified-source theology;
   the System is a counterfeit. Narnia-model, never preached. The book's reason
   to exist.
2. **Omniscient Storyteller** *(BEND the first-person default)* — most LitRPG is
   first-person or tight-third only; forge-novel adds the warm omniscient Brigid
   narrator (two registers, Hiberno-English omniscient).
3. **Audiobook-primary, full-cast** *(BREAK the page-first default)* — the
   audiobook is the deliverable and the **master tiebreaker**; most genre entries
   treat audio as an afterthought.
4. **Interface restraint** *(BEND crunch maximalism)* — fighter-pilot HUD,
   ≤6-line stat blocks, numbers-as-atmosphere, effects-not-UI. The
   anti-Primal-Hunter dial, toward Cradle.

Everything else on this page is a HONOR or an honor-with-twist. If a beat reads as
breaking one of the four above, confirm it's *this* break and intended. If a beat
breaks a convention **not** on this list, that's the bug.

## Editor's genre-contract check (light pass)

A short awareness pass `/editors-hat` can run alongside the others — not a full
pass, a scan for *unintended* breaks:

- **Stat-sheet bloat** — any block over ~6 lines, or a paragraph reading like a
  spreadsheet → flag (BEND violated).
- **Mid-action level-up** — any "ding" / level-up / stat gain interrupting a beat
  instead of landing at a scene/chapter seam → flag.
- **Loadout-optimization scene** — Nate deliberating a build over multiple beats
  → flag (this is a recorded BREAK; honoring it is the error).
- **System-as-neutral-tech drift** — prose treating the System as alien tech or
  neutral game-engine rather than a counterfeit → flag (theology spine).
- **First-person leak** — a stretch that loses the Storyteller entirely and reads
  as flat genre first-person → flag (the omniscient narrator is a signature).

A *recorded* break is correct and needs no action. Only an **unrecorded** stance
shift is a finding.

## Writer's consult (forge-write)

Before drafting a beat that leans on a convention — a level-up, a loot drop, a
class reveal, a stat readout, a tutorial-room beat — check this doc's stance:

- **Deliver the reader's need even when the form bends.** A BEND still pays off
  the underlying expectation (growth stays visible; the milestone still lands) —
  just by forge-novel's means.
- **Never honor a convention forge-novel deliberately breaks.** Don't drift into
  spreadsheet crunch, notification spam, loadout-optimization, or secular
  power-fantasy framing because "that's what the genre does." The four signature
  divergences above are load-bearing.

## Kit portability

This file is the **swappable genre layer.** To retarget the toolkit at another
genre, replace the conventions and stances here; the three-layer split
(canon / craft / **genre-contract**) and the HONOR / BEND / BREAK method carry
over unchanged.
