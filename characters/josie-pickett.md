## Physical Description

5'4", compact, solid. Not petite — *dense.* The build of a woman who's hauled furniture, loaded trucks, and carried boxes since she was old enough to help.

- Dark brown hair, almost black. Practical ponytail — always. She doesn't think about her hair. She thinks about what her hands are doing.
- Brown eyes. Quick — they don't rest on anything, they *scan.* When she walks into a room, her eyes do an inventory sweep before she's consciously aware of it.
- Tanned from loading trucks in parking lots and setting up tables at outdoor flea markets in East Texas heat.
- Usually has a smudge of something on her. Dust, grease, marker ink from pricing stickers. Clean clothes last about twenty minutes on Josie Pickett.

---

## Family

**Dad — Roy Pickett.** Works at the lumber yard in Marshall. Quiet, steady, does his job, comes home. Thinks Josie's estate sale obsession is "a phase." (She still has the rock collection from when she was nine.)

**Mom — Darlene Pickett (née Hooks).** Front desk at a veterinary clinic in Marshall. Warmer than Roy. Worries about Josie living in a cramped apartment above a pawn shop. Sends casseroles she doesn't need and advice she doesn't take.

**No siblings.** Only child. The manic energy isn't competing with anyone for attention — it's just how she's built.

**Functional, loving, working-class family.** No trauma. No estrangement. Two parents who don't quite understand their daughter and love her anyway.

At Integration: Roy and Darlene are in Marshall. Josie is at an estate sale outside Carthage. Different Tutorial pockets. She doesn't know if they're alive.

## Pre-Integration Life

**The Pawn Shop — "Second Chances"**
Main drag in Marshall. Not hers — she works the counter. Owner hired her because she could identify a genuine Colt SAA from a reproduction at nineteen. She runs the estate sale side — scouts sales, buys inventory, prices it, moves it. She's the profit engine the owner doesn't fully appreciate.

Saving to open her own shop. At Integration she had $4,200 in savings and a business plan in a spiral notebook. Neither of those things matter anymore.

**The Apartment**
Rents a tiny place above the pawn shop. Full — not hoarder-full, inventory-full. Organized chaos. Every surface has something she's appraising, researching, or waiting to sell. She knows where everything is. Nobody else does.

**At Integration**
Saturday morning. A deceased farmer's estate sale outside Carthage — whole barn full of equipment, three generations of accumulation. She'd been watching the listing for two weeks. She was in the barn when the sky broke.

## Core Worldview

**Everything has value if you know how to look.**

The rusty toolbox at a farm estate that everyone walks past? Complete set of 1960s Craftsman wrenches inside. The ugly painting at the thrift store? Covering a better painting underneath. The broken clock radio? The capacitors are worth more than the radio ever was.

She learned this from doing. A thousand Saturday mornings at estate sales and flea markets. Sorting through dead people's lives and finding the treasure they didn't know they had. It sounds morbid. To Josie it's *respectful* — she's the person who sees what the deceased cared about when nobody else bothers to look.

This is why the Tutorial doesn't break her. The world ended. Monsters are real. She's in a dungeon. But the dungeon is full of *materials,* and materials have value, and value is what Josie Pickett understands better than anything else in her life.

## Personality

- Manic energy — talks too fast because her brain inventories faster than her mouth can keep up
- Catastrophically sincere about material waste in life-or-death situations
- Deeply, personally offended when anyone discards useful materials
- Not performing humor — funny because she genuinely believes the thorn matters more than the fight
- Stream-of-consciousness appraisal that sounds chaotic but is actually precise
- Talks to materials like they're pets

**Signature line (organic, not forced):** "Are you just going to LEAVE that there?"

---

## System Class — Appraiser (Codex #103)

The System read her aptitude for identifying and valuing objects and assigned a support class.

**Skills:**
- Material Identification — sees composition, quality grade, hidden properties
- Value Assessment — knows what's rare before the System classifies it
- Salvage Optimization — extracts more usable material from drops than other classes

**Progression:** At F-rank: basic quality grades and material types. At higher ranks: hidden properties, pre-System origins, items the System's own classification missed.

---

## Combat Role

Nearly zero. Not a fighter. Survives the Tutorial by being compact, fast, and too busy looting corpses for anyone to notice she's not swinging a sword. Her value is entirely in what she brings BACK from the fight.

---

## Comedy Function

Primary comic relief alongside Flint. Complementary styles:
- **Flint:** Sharp, dry, top-down sarcasm (precision from above)
- **Josie:** Manic, earnest, bottom-up enthusiasm (sincerity from below)

The humor works on three levels:
1. **Contrast** — life-or-death situation, she's upset about material waste
2. **Sincerity** — not performing. She genuinely believes the thorn matters.
3. **Competence** — she's actually RIGHT. The comedy earns respect.

---

## The Flint-Josie Dynamic

They argue about materials while people fight for their lives. Flint is technically superior. Josie is practically superior. Neither will ever admit the other is right first.

Flint eventually develops "The Pickett Variable" — a factor he adds to his calculations that accounts for her finding things that shouldn't exist.

Flint's first assessment: "The small loud one has grabbed seventeen pieces of biological debris in the last four minutes. I want to classify this as a behavioral anomaly but I think it might just be a personality."

---

## Relationship to Nate

Respects him immediately because he's the first person she meets who ALSO sees value in things other people ignore. When Nate picks materials instead of a weapon in Room 0, Josie is the one person in the room who understands exactly what he's doing.

Her Appraiser vision and his Structural Analysis complement each other — she sees what something is WORTH, he sees what something IS. The crafting dream team the System didn't plan for.

---

## Long-Term Arc

Survives the Tutorial. Joins Nate's settlement. Becomes the quartermaster and scavenger. As her Appraiser rank grows, starts seeing things the System's loot tables don't list — pre-System materials, divine architecture fragments in mundane objects. Becomes one of the first people to bring Nate materials he can forge with.

---

## Faith Status and Redemption Path

**System-integrated.** Carries the System's hooks through progression like everyone else.

Not faithless — just not paying attention. Grew up going to church because her parents went — Easter, Christmas, the occasional Sunday when Darlene guilted her into it. Not hostile to faith. Not seeking it. Just busy. The System gave her a class that does what she already does, but better. Why question that?

**The Crack** comes from watching **Nate** — not from her own class. She sees a man whose power doesn't come from the System do things the System says are impossible. Her Appraiser brain can price anything. But Nate is something she can't appraise. The woman who sees value in everything encounters something she can't quantify. That's the question that starts her listening.

**Timing:** Probably Book 3-4.

The narrative power: the reader loves Josie before they realize the strings are on her too. Her conversion carries weight because the reader is already invested.
