## Personality

- Manic energy — talks too fast because her brain inventories faster than her mouth can keep up
- Catastrophically sincere about material waste in life-or-death situations
- Deeply, personally offended when anyone discards useful materials
- Not performing humor — funny because she genuinely believes the thorn matters more than the fight
- Stream-of-consciousness appraisal that sounds chaotic but is actually precise
- Talks to materials like they're pets
- The person at every farm auction and estate sale who knows what everything is worth and will not shut up about it

---

## Pre-Integration Life

Runs or works at a pawn shop / estate sale operation in the Panola County area. Grew up in the rural East Texas economy of estate sales, flea markets, antique malls. Knows what things are worth when nobody else does. Can price a Depression-era cast iron skillet at thirty paces. Knows which junk drawer at a dead farmer's estate has the $400 pocket knife. Seeing value where others see trash is a survival skill in her world.

---

## System Class — Appraiser

The System read her aptitude for identifying and valuing objects and assigned a support class.

**Skills:**
- Material Identification — sees composition, quality grade, hidden properties
- Value Assessment — knows what's rare before the System classifies it
- Salvage Optimization — extracts more usable material from drops than other classes

**Progression:** At F-rank: basic quality grades and material types. At higher ranks: hidden properties, pre-System origins, items the System's own classification missed.

---

## Combat Role

Nearly zero. Not a fighter. Survives the Tutorial by staying behind the line, being small and fast, and being too busy looting corpses to present as a target. Her value is entirely in what she brings BACK from the fight. Every rest area, she dumps armfuls of materials on the crafting station and makes the crafters' eyes light up.

---

## Comedy Function

Primary comic relief alongside Flint. Complementary comedy styles:
- **Flint:** Sharp, dry, top-down sarcasm (precision from above)
- **Josie:** Manic, earnest, bottom-up enthusiasm (sincerity from below)

The humor works on three levels:
1. **Contrast** — life-or-death situation, she's upset about material waste
2. **Sincerity** — not performing. She genuinely believes the thorn matters.
3. **Competence** — she's actually RIGHT. The comedy earns respect.

**Signature line (organic, not forced):** "Are you just going to LEAVE that there?"

---

## The Flint-Josie Dynamic

They argue about materials while people fight for their lives. Flint is technically superior. Josie is practically superior. Neither will ever admit the other is right first. Flint eventually develops "The Pickett Variable" — a factor he adds to his calculations that accounts for her finding things that shouldn't exist in the System's loot tables.

Flint's first assessment: "The small loud one has grabbed seventeen pieces of biological debris in the last four minutes. I want to classify this as a behavioral anomaly but I think it might just be a personality."

---

## Long-Term Arc

Survives the Tutorial. Joins Nate's settlement. Becomes the quartermaster and scavenger — the person who makes sure nothing gets wasted and everything gets catalogued. As her Appraiser rank grows, she starts seeing things the System's own loot tables don't list — pre-System materials, divine architecture fragments in mundane objects, items the System classified as junk that are actually significant. She becomes one of the first people to bring Nate materials he can forge with.

---

## Faith Status

**System-integrated.** She carries the System's strings like everyone else. Potential Redemption Path candidate (Entity #59) later in the series. The narrative power: the reader loves Josie before they realize the strings are on her too. Her eventual conversion (if/when it happens) carries weight because the reader has already invested in her.

---

## Relationship to Nate

Respects him immediately because he's the first person she meets who ALSO sees value in things other people ignore. When Nate picks materials instead of a weapon in Room 0, Josie is the one person in the room who understands exactly what he's doing. She doesn't know why. She just knows he sees like she sees.

Her Appraiser vision and his Structural Analysis complement each other — she sees what something is WORTH, he sees what something IS. The crafting dream team the System didn't plan for.
