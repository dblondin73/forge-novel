# HUD Visual Design Spec — Beneath the Overlay

*Reference for prose formatting. Forge Codex Entity #75.*

---

## What the HUD IS NOT

- NOT System-built. The System did not create it and cannot access it.
- NOT a blue popup screen. No tooltips, no windows, no gaming UI.
- NOT a bionic implant. No hardware. No cybernetics.
- NOT removable. Cannot be turned off.

## What the HUD IS

The conduit's interface, rendered through Nate's engineering brain as a fighter-pilot overlay. Data integrated into vision — seen THROUGH, not AT. Hard geometry: triangles, sharp angles, pronged shapes. Aerospace aesthetic.

Affected by the world: flickers near strong System constructs, drops 0.3 seconds during Spirit moments, sharpens in weak-overlay zones, shakes during impacts.

---

## Color Language

| Color | Meaning | Phase |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Monochrome green | Base data (health, stamina, stats) | Phase 1+ |
| Amber/orange | Threat indicators, danger proximity | Phase 2+ |
| Blue-green | Environmental data (terrain, water, structure) | Phase 2+ |
| Cold white | System processes, rune strings | Phase 2+ |
| Luminous gold | Divine architecture (weak-overlay zones) | Phase 2+ |

---

## Phase Progression

### Phase 1 — BASIC (Book 1, early)
- Monochrome green, faint, barely noticeable
- Health and stamina only — two thin angular bars at lower periphery
- No numbers. Just bar length = percentage
- "A faint green shimmer at the edge of his vision, so subtle he thought it was an afterimage from staring at the sun."

### Phase 2 — TACTICAL (Book 1, Ch22+)
- Color arrives. Green base + amber threats + blue-green environmental + cold white System
- All core stats visible
- Threat indicators: amber/red triangles at periphery pointing toward threats
- THE STRINGS visible: thin white threads from every System rune back to central point
- Divine rune patterns visible in weak-overlay zones — luminous lines through stone like water
- "The world had color now. Not the color of paint or screens — the color of threat assessment."

### Phase 3 — ANALYTICAL (Books 3-4)
- Structural analysis overlay
- Crafting identification
- System process visibility at depth
- Basic rune reading
- Labyrinth architecture detail beneath overlay

### Phase 4 — ARCHITECTURAL (Books 5-7)
- Full dual-layer vision: counterfeit and real simultaneously
- Divine notation fluency
- Full Labyrinth architecture visible beneath System overlay

## Prose Formatting Rules

DO: Describe HUD data experientially.
- "The green bar at his periphery had dropped to half."
- "Amber flared at the edge of his left eye — something approaching from the tree line."
- "Cold white threads ran from the rune on the blade, thin as spider silk, trailing off toward something he couldn't see yet."

DO NOT: Use screen-readout formatting.
- Not "Health: 50%"
- Not "[THREAT DETECTED — Level 8 Wolf, Northwest]"
- Not a boxed stat display in the middle of prose

EXCEPTION: System classification text appears in italics inline when the System tries and fails to categorize Nate:
- PROCESSING
- ERROR — EXCEEDS MEASUREMENT RANGE
- INTEGRATION ANOMALY — STANDARD CLASSIFICATION: FAILED

These are the System's language breaking through — mechanical, cold, frustrated. They contrast with the warm organic feel of the HUD itself.

Glitch moments: Describe as visual disruption — static, flicker, brief blindness, color shift. Not software error messages.

Critical rule: The HUD never talks. It displays. Flint interprets. HUD = visual data. Flint = verbal analysis. Keep them distinct.
