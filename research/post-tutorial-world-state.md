# Post-Tutorial World State — Infrastructure Reference

*Locked: Session 014 | Codex Entities: #111, #112*

---

## THE COLLAPSE MODEL

**Seductive replacement, not destruction.**

The System didn't bomb anything. The physical infrastructure of civilization is intact on Day 1. The System is a SOFTWARE overlay on HARDWARE that already existed. What breaks is the OPERATORS — people stop maintaining the old systems because the new ones are more convenient.

The System doesn't need to destroy civilization. It needs to **replace** it.

---

## SPECIFIC SYSTEMS

### Electricity
- Grid fails within 1-2 weeks (no operators, no fuel delivery)
- Military installations maintain power through generators
- Rural properties with generators and fuel hold longer
- System alternative: Mage light, fire skills — free, convenient, tracked
- Nate's answer: diesel generator (Book 1), divine runes (Book 2+)

### Fuel / Gasoline
- What's in the tanks is all there is
- Refineries not running — too complex, too many operators needed
- Gasoline degrades 6-12 months. Diesel lasts longer.
- Within a year, combustion engines are dying
- System alternative: movement skills, teleportation quests
- Nate's answer: conserve diesel, eventually rune-powered infrastructure

### Water
- Municipal treatment fails with the grid
- Electric well pumps die without power
- Springs, gravity-fed systems, hand pumps survive
- Nate's ranch: spring-fed stock tank (gravity) + well pump (Field Repair → rune-powered)
- Water is the FIRST infrastructure problem. "Water first."

### Food
- Supply chains broken. Grocery stores emptied within days.
- Agriculture continues where farmers still farm — but many are grinding XP
- Ranches with livestock are food-secure if they can defend the herd
- System alternative: food through quests and dungeon drops — another dependency hook
- Nate's answer: cattle, garden, trade with other settlements

### Communication
- Cell towers die with the grid
- System messaging = only reliable comms = System controls the phone lines
- Amateur radio becomes critical for independent settlements
- Nate's Flint: receive-only in Book 1. Can hear, can't call back.

## SETTLEMENT PHILOSOPHY

**Anti-counterfeit, NOT anti-magic.**

Nate uses magic. He uses it the way the SOURCE of his power intended. The rune system is a power source — divine runes applied to infrastructure replace conventional power. A rune on a water pump draws from divine architecture instead of the grid. Clean. Permanent. Invisible to the System.

System runes can do the same thing — but every System rune is a surveillance antenna and a kill switch.

## TIMELINE

**Week 1-2:** Power spotty, communication fragmenting, violence local, most people confused with blue screens.

**Month 1-3:** Grid mostly dead, gasoline dwindling, System communities forming (class hierarchies, quest economies, Patron territories). Independent communities forming around real infrastructure.

**Year 1+:** Hybrid society — System magic + maintained human tech. The System gradually makes itself indispensable. Fewer people remember how to run a generator each year.

## KEY TENSION

Nate can only produce so many divine-runed items. The settlement needs more infrastructure than he can rune alone. Does he accept System-runed equipment to fill the gaps? Every System rune is a hook. This is the ongoing series dilemma.
