## The Comedy Formula

Josie's humor works on three levels:
1. **Contrast** — life-or-death situation, she's upset about material waste
2. **Sincerity** — not performing. She genuinely believes the thorn matters more than the fight.
3. **Competence** — she's actually RIGHT. Every time someone dismisses her, the materials turn out critical.

She is **catastrophically sincere.** The world is ending and she is UPSET about people stepping on perfectly good thorns.

## Nate Watching Josie Work

She talked while she worked. Not to anyone — to the materials. Josie Pickett sorted Thornling parts the way some people sorted laundry, if laundry could stab you and your sorting method involved a running monologue about grain density.

"You're a load-bearer. You're decorative trash. You... I don't know what you are yet but you're coming with me. And YOU — " she held up a thorn that had a slight curve to it, examined it under the torchlight like a jeweler with a diamond, and made a sound that was uncomfortably close to affection. "You're *special.*"

The Warrior who'd kicked a Thornguard corpse earlier was watching her with the expression of a man who had just realized the weird girl was the most important person in the room and did not know how to feel about it.

## Flint-Josie First Clash

"That thorn isn't structural. The fracture pattern indicates it was a secondary growth — decorative, not load-bearing."

Josie looked up from her sorting pile and stared at Nate. "Excuse me?"

Nate sighed. He hated when Flint did this. "My... system... is telling me that thorn is decorative."

"Your *system* is wrong." She held the thorn up. "See this ridge along the base? That's a stress line. Stress lines don't form on decorative growths because decorative growths don't bear stress. This thorn was holding something together, which means it's denser than the others, which means it holds an edge longer, which means your system can take its opinion and — "

"She's right," Flint said internally, with the specific tone of an AI that would rather eat its own code than admit it.

"She's right," Nate said out loud.

Josie pointed at him with the thorn. "I know."
