## Purpose

Establish the **Labyrinth connection point that manifests on Nate's ranch** during
Integration: what it is, what it gives him, what it costs him, and how he comes to
control it. The node is the spine that ties three existing systems together —

1. The **Labyrinth-bleed** mechanic (terrain/material/fauna drift toward the nearest
   connected world; see `project_forge_earth_labyrinth_bleed`)
2. The **Veil-break release** mechanic (a connection unseals pocket dimensions,
   dungeons, and connection points, loosing monsters/animals/people; see
   `project_veil_break_releases`)
3. The **Cornerstone settlement** system (land-binding anchor; see
   [cornerstone-settlement-design.md](cornerstone-settlement-design.md), Codex #149)

— into a single piece of ground that is simultaneously Nate's **home, supply, siege,
and quest.** It is the engine of the early book and a fixture of the series geography.

---

## Cosmology framing

Anchor for every decision below. The node is **clean-channel provision.** The Labyrinth
is the Creator's pipeline (Codex #177–179); the resources, terraforming, and the
dungeon itself are Labyrinth-native and Creator-sourced. The **System overlay** did not
make any of it — at most it will later try to *meter* what it didn't create (see Open
Threads). "Becoming dungeon master" is therefore **stewardship and dominion**, not
evil-overlord lordship: Nate exercises rightful authority over restored ground, never a
class the System sells him. The diagnostic test from the Cornerstone doc still applies —
*"work for it, alignment matters, no shortcut"* is clean Labyrinth; *"buy this faster,
accept this binding"* is System scaffolding. The node is the former.

**Never in prose:** "node," "clean channel," "Labyrinth pipeline," "enriched-native,"
"dominion mandate," or any cosmology label. Render through effect (Narnia Principle).

## 1. What the node is

A **permanent Labyrinth connection / dungeon mouth** that opens on Nate's land in the
Integration. It is a real place — a real dungeon below, not an overlay (per
`project_labyrinth_expanded_design`), with node fast-travel potential later and a
Greek-over-divine-bones aesthetic deeper in.

**Seed location.** Best dramatic seeding is a place Nate *already knew*, so the change
lands as gift **and** violation — "the land he understood became something it never
was." Reconcile with the pinned ranch (Panola County, Piney Woods; see
[east-texas-soil.md](east-texas-soil.md)):

- **Use** a creek-bottom outcrop / sandstone-or-ironstone bank in the bottomland
  hardwoods, or the ground near the existing north-pasture wellhead (already on the
  page, Ch1/Ch3).
- **Avoid** the "old iron-ore mine working" I floated in early discussion — historic
  iron mining was Cherokee/Rusk County to the west, **not** Panola. Don't put a mine
  adit on his land; it contradicts the pinned location.

## 2. Permanence — node-anchored stable zone  *(Decision 1)*

**Decision:** The terraformed ground **around the node is permanent** — stable as long
as the connection stays open. The diffuse Labyrinth-bleed farther out still recedes per
existing canon as Earth equilibrates to its own post-Integration identity.

**Consequences:**

- His ranch becomes a **standing resource node** from day one — and therefore a
  **permanent strategic target.** Other survivors, factions, and eventually the
  System's people will want it. Built-in series-long conflict engine.
- The resources flow only while the node lives, giving Nate a concrete reason to
  **keep the connection open and defend it.** Defense is his supply line, not an
  abstraction.
- The node sits **co-located with the Cornerstone** — anchor and node on the same
  ground. The Cornerstone arc (Codex #149) gains its physical center here.

---

## 3. Two-zone resource model  *(Decision 2)*

The node yields on two layers, deliberately separated so the **settlement track feeds
from the safe-ish surface** and the **power track feeds from the dangerous deep** — and
both come from the same ground, so building up home and pushing into the dungeon are one
strategic project, not competing ones.

### 3a. Surface / surrounding zone — the economy layer (enriched-native)

The terraforming **transmutes the native material into its better self.** This is the
key reconciliation with [east-texas-soil.md](east-texas-soil.md): Panola County's red
clay is iron *oxide*, not ore — diffuse, un-smeltable, "dirt that blushes." The node
does what millennia of weathering could not — it **concentrates the diffuse iron into a
real, workable vein**, and hardens the crumbly Carrizo-Wilcox sandstone into
building-grade stone (pale and dense, near dressed-limestone).

- **Native (pre-node):** sandy loam over red clay, loblolly/shortleaf pine, marginal
  ironstone — good timber, **no building stone, no smeltable ore.**
- **Enriched (post-node):** real **iron ore** + **building-grade stone.** The node
  supplies exactly the two things the Piney Woods lacks; timber he already had.
- **How he gets it:** **labor/quarry-gated.** He works it by hand with his tools and
  his Structural Analysis maker's eye — no danger gate, but no free pickup either.
- **Plausibility:** stays grounded; never raises a "why is there marble in Texas"
  eyebrow. The stone reads as upgraded local rock, not imported exotica.

### 3b. Dungeon interior — the adventure layer (exotic)

Inside the threshold: **exotic materials, gems, rare ores** — the connected world's
substance undiluted, gated behind delving risk, **deeper = rarer** (standard dungeon
gradient, treated as default). This is the premium crafting/progression stock he
*earns*, distinct from the grounded building stock outside.

---

## 4. Contested frontier + leak cap  *(Decisions 3 & 4)*

**Decision 3 — Contested frontier:** The node **leaks threats outward** (Veil-break
canon). The surface zone is a **live frontier** — Nate harvests and builds *under
threat* from day one. No quiet refuge; the ranch is a position he *holds*, not a haven
he retreats to. This commits the early book to **sustained forward pressure**, which
lines up with the LitRPG momentum rule (`feedback_litrpg_pacing`).

**Decision 4 — Leak cap:** Only **starter-level creatures** clear the portal. Everything
stronger is **held inside by the entrance portal/seal.** This is the keystone that makes
the contested frontier survivable instead of suicidal — the frontier stays *hot* but the
escaping threats are weak; all the real danger remains sealed below.

**Why the two interlock:** the leak cap turns the dungeon into a **goal.** The dangerous
creatures are *in there*, and the only way to stop the leak permanently is to go in and
take the seal. The pressure (leak) and the prize (mastery) are the **same object.**

**Frontline consequence — the dogs and the herd.** A leaking node beside livestock is
constant pressure. Rex and Judge stop being color and become **frontline sentries**; the
pack-bond becomes a defense faculty, not just warmth (Codex #170 / #136). The cattle are
immediately in play.

---

## 5. Conquer-to-master  *(Decision 5)*

**Decision:** Nate must **conquer the dungeon to become its dungeon master.** Taking the
dungeon = taking the seal = **stopping the leak permanently AND gaining mastery in the
same act.** Mastery flows through **alignment / the clean channel** (Conduit, Codex
#178), *not* a class the System grants — inverting the usual "the System makes you
Dungeon Lord" beat. His authority is a function of *who he serves.*

**The node arms its own siege** — a clean, grim image to lean on: the stone he pulls
from the surface goes straight into the walls he raises against the things that came up
with it. Building material and threat issue from the same hole.

**Open (Decision 5b, deferred):** *what mastery actually grants.* Candidate levers —
control over respawns, over the layout, a draw on the dungeon's output, a "seat/heart"
he holds. This is where the crunch lives; settle it when the dungeon-conquest beat is on
the drafting table. See Open Threads.

## 6. Genre pedigree (where this comes from, how it stays distinct)

David's stated source is **The Land / Chaos Seeds** (Aleron Kong) — *Places of Power*
that grant great power to whoever becomes their **Master**; Richter masters the Mist
Village and gains a dungeon. The broader family, for reference:

- **Game ur-text:** *Dungeon Keeper* (1997) — the player *is* the dungeon master who
  builds and defends a dungeon against invading heroes. Grandfather of the trope.
- **Western dungeon-core LitRPG:** *Dungeon Lord — The Wraith's Haunt* (Hugo Huesca) is
  the on-the-nose title (protagonist *becomes a Dungeon Lord*). Note the subgenre split:
  most dungeon-core books make you *the dungeon itself* (*Dungeon Born / The Divine
  Dungeon*, *Bone Dungeon*, *Threadbare*) — **not** our model. Nate stays Nate and
  *takes* a dungeon, the rarer and stronger version.
- **Adjacent conquer-and-command:** *Solo Leveling* (clear the dungeon, command its
  defeated monsters), *Overlord* (rules a tomb-dungeon with guardian NPCs).
- **Cultivation parallel — the deep cut.** Xianxia's **dongtian / dongfu**
  ("grotto-heaven" / "cave-abode"): an energy-dense secret realm a powerful cultivator
  **claims as a personal cultivation ground, stronghold, and power source.** This is
  structurally identical to Nate claiming the node. Because the series fuses cultivation
  in, **Nate's node = LitRPG dungeon-lordship welded to xianxia cave-heaven** — fused
  from two traditions, not borrowed from one.

**What keeps it from being a clone** (none of the above run all three together):

1. The dungeon is a **Labyrinth connection — the Creator's clean pipeline** — not a
   demon-dug lair. Stewardship/dominion, never evil-overlord. A theological reframe of
   the whole trope.
2. Mastery flows through **alignment**, not a System-granted class.
3. The **resource-node-on-his-own-homestead + starter-leak-cap + conquer-to-seal**
   configuration is a specific tuning The Land never runs.

This is consistent with the Cornerstone doc's stance: forge-novel renders settlement/
dungeon mechanics as **Labyrinth-native pre-Veil infrastructure coming back online** —
canonical, not derivative. The genre converged on the right shape because the shape is
the right shape.

## 7. Story consequences (keep in view while drafting forward)

- **The cattle and the dogs are in play immediately.** Constant pressure beside
  livestock; Rex and Judge become sentries; the pack-bond turns load-bearing.
- **Defense is the first construction priority** — he fortifies *as* he harvests. The
  node arms its own siege.
- **The Cornerstone goes from upgrade to survival** — anchoring the land is how he keeps
  the one position that feeds him, not an optimization.
- **It justifies the crunch.** Stat sheets, building tiers, and the settlement/power
  tracks earn their keep here — Nate has a concrete reason to measure everything (low-
  moderate crunch per the progression bible and Cornerstone crunch ceiling).

## 8. Open threads

- **(5b) What dungeon-mastery grants Nate** — respawn control, layout control, a draw on
  output, a held "seat/heart." Settle when the conquest beat drafts. *This is the next
  design beat.*
- **System metering of the node** — the Labyrinth provides freely (clean channel); the
  System overlay would plausibly try to **tax/gate/charge rent** on provision it didn't
  make (the counterfeit claiming ownership of the real thing). Rich **Book 2+** conflict
  hook; **not** load-bearing for Book 1. Parked, not decided.
- **Connection's destination world** — which world the node bridges to flavors the
  interior's exotic palette and the corridor aesthetic (corridor look shifts by world
  proximity; see `project_labyrinth_corridor_aesthetics`). Open.
- **Timing** — when in Book 1 the node manifests, and how it sequences against the
  Tutorial arc and the cohort scatter. Open; coordinate with the outline.
