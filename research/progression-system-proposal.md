## III. The unified system

### Level
**What it is:** the System's **class-progression odometer** — how far along the Engineer chassis you are. A *schedule*, not a power stat (D&D class-level-as-tier-spine): each Level slot paces **which** class skills/abilities unlock and **when**.

**How it grows:** **milestone-paced** (story beats: the resource-split sacrifice, the boss-made-killable beat). Frequent enough to be the steady dopamine drip; Book 1 = Level 4 (Tutorial exit) → 10 (book end).

**Death tax — #149 translated into the milestone model (FLAG for David).** Codex #149 says death loses "levels (5% penalty per death)." A 5% tax is a percentage of a **continuous** XP-style pool; it does **not** compute cleanly against a discrete milestone ladder (5% of Level 10 = 0.5 levels, meaningless). **Two ways to reconcile, David's call:**
- *(i)* Level keeps an underlying continuous XP-like quantity (so 5% is computable) — but then "milestone-paced, not tallied XP" is wrong and Level becomes a normal XP bar.
- *(ii)* Death costs a **flat Level-step** (drop one Level / one milestone), and #149's "5% penalty" is restated as "one Level step."
**Recommend (ii)** — preserves the milestone model and keeps Level off the page as a felt setback, not an arithmetic one. *This needs David's ruling before #149 propagates.*

**How it differs from Rank:** Level is the *System's bookkeeping number* (scaffolding; binding hooks attach here).
