## Purpose

Establish a cosmology-aligned settlement-and-base-building system for forge-novel that:

1. Preserves the locked **Labyrinth-native = clean / System = parasitic shortcuts** cosmology — settlement gameplay isn't an "import from The Land" or any other LitRPG, it's Earth finally regaining access to mechanics that always existed pre-Veil
2. Renders Nate's ranch-anchored Cornerstone arc with **low-moderate crunch** (Flint surfaces a few numeric highlights; the rest is somatic / relational)
3. Makes the Conduit-side / System-side contrast **visible by mechanic shape**, not by narration
4. Stays compatible with the locked Tutorial cohort scatter, the Bezalel-pattern Conduit Gift, the pack-bond relational faculty, and the Two-Faculty Model

The world-bench scales from Nate-alone-with-dogs (Stage 1, most of Book 1) to hamlet (Stage 2, Book 2 cohort regather) to village (Stage 3, Book 2 late / Book 3). The novel's spotlight follows.

---

## Cosmology framing

Anchor for every design decision below.

### Labyrinth-native settlement mechanics

All LitRPG-type settlement infrastructure — building tiers, recruitable populations, settlement state, territory radius, work-economies — is **Labyrinth-native and Creator-sourced.** It existed before the Veil sealed Earth off. Integration restored Earth's access to it. The mechanics aren't new inventions; they are pre-Veil infrastructure coming back online.

The genre converged on the right shape because the shape *is* the right shape. Kong, Sisa, Chatfield, et al. each sketched at it independently. Forge-novel renders it as canonical, not derivative.

### System as parasitic shortcut layer

**The Adversary** (Lucifer ≡ Beelzebub — interchangeable names for the same fallen entity in this cosmology) cannot create Labyrinth-native infrastructure. The System's contribution is **contract-binding shortcuts** layered on top of the clean infrastructure:

- **Pay-to-win mechanics** — spendable currencies (Research Points, paid blueprints, ascension tokens) that accelerate progress in exchange for binding-depth (Codex #100 stages)
- **Patron-anchored buildings** — structures whose bonuses come from a fallen-being bond rather than from faithful use (Codex #7)
- **Branded conveniences** — the Morningstar Group (MSG) corporate face that delivers System-routed services with smiling forms and signing bonuses (locked per the MSG brand entry)

**The diagnostic test for any settlement mechanic in this design:** does it feel like *"buy this faster, accept this binding"* or like *"work for it, alignment matters, no shortcut available"*? The first is System scaffolding. The second is clean Labyrinth.

## 1. Crunch ceiling — Hybrid Flint highlights

**Decision:** Flint surfaces 1-2 named numeric metrics per beat as one-line HUD pings; Nate feels the rest somatically through Cornerstone-bond. No high-crunch tables, no permanent overlay panels, no audit-grade tracking.

**Format example:**

> *Cornerstone resonance: stable. Cohesion ticked up two.*
>
> Then back to scene.

**What Flint surfaces:**

- Cornerstone resonance status (one-word state: stable / rising / pressed / fading)
- One delta number per beat when relevant (cohesion ticked up two; provision dropped four)
- Pack-bond state (Stage 1 only, since Rex and Judge are the entire Stage 1 population)

**What Flint does NOT surface:**

- Multi-row stat tables
- Per-soul individual readouts in normal scenes
- Building-quality grids or research-progress bars
- Constant background overlay (panels appear when Nate explicitly checks, then dismiss)

**Rationale:**

- Matches locked low-moderate crunch ceiling per [project_forge_progression_and_flint_arc](../../../Personal/CLAUDE.md) memory
- Mirrors the use-based skill-rank precedent — numbers as a translation layer, not a control panel
- Two-Faculty Model alignment: the analytical faculty (Flint) reports the metric; the relational faculty (Nate's bond with the land) generates the actual state. The number is downstream of the relationship
- Atmosphere-numbers principle ([feedback_prose_numbers_are_atmosphere](../../../Personal/CLAUDE.md)) — specific-sounding numbers do rhetorical work, not audit-grade headcount

## 2. Progression — Twin Track

**Decision:** Conduit-side Cornerstone settlements progress on two parallel tracks. Both required to advance.

### Track A — SA Insight (what to build)

Nate's **Bezalel-pattern Conduit amplification** over Structural Analysis reveals what the Cornerstone is ready to receive. He sees the next structure when alignment + readiness intersect — load paths for a building that doesn't exist yet, materials he doesn't have but will, the shape of what wants to be built.

- Insight is **Spirit-amplified** (Conduit Gift overlay per [magic_system.md](../prompts/magic_system.md) Codex #178)
- Aligned state allows insight; Doubt plateaus it; Opposition silences it
- Insight is the *vision* — what *can* be next

### Track B — Cornerstone Resonance (when ready)

Resonance is the Cornerstone's covenantal bond with the land and souls under its anchor. It accrues through **faithful use** — lives lived on the land, animals tended, work done in alignment, hospitality given. No spendable currency; no XP allocation. The pattern mirrors the use-based skill-rank mechanic.

- Resonance is **relational-faculty** state (the land's pack-bond with its anchor + people)
- Faithful living in alignment grows it; opposition stalls it (stasis, not decay — same model as Conduit overlay)
- Resonance is the *readiness* — when next is *available*

### Both required

Insight without resonance: Nate sees what could be, but the land isn't ready — building it won't take, materials won't bind, the structure will read as scaffolding rather than installation.

Resonance without insight: the Cornerstone is ready to grow, but Nate doesn't yet see what should grow. Time on the land brings clarity.

**No spendable currency exists clean-side.** Any spendable settlement-currency mechanic in this novel is a System construct (Research-Point-equivalents, contract tokens, MSG paid blueprints). That distinction is load-bearing for the Conduit/System contrast.

## 3. Activation — Three-stage population-gated

**Decision:** Settlement-state surfacing is gated by both population AND Cornerstone resonance tier. Neither alone advances the stage.

### Stage 1 — Ranch (1-5 souls)

- **Active metrics:** Cornerstone resonance only
- **Population:** Nate alone + Rex + Judge (canonical at end of Tutorial); maybe one or two cohort members re-arriving by end of Book 1
- **Most of Book 1 sits here.** This is the foundation stage — the bond between Nate, the pack, the Cornerstone, and the bonded acreage
- **Pack-bond carries Stage 1's relational load.** Per [feedback_rex_bond_bidirectional](../../../Personal/CLAUDE.md), Rex (beta) and Judge (omega) are pack core. At 1-soul-plus-2-dogs the only meaningful settlement-state Flint can report is Cornerstone resonance + pack state — and the existing relational-faculty design is already saying that

### Stage 2 — Hamlet (5-20 souls)

- **Active metrics:** Cornerstone resonance + Cohesion + Provision (see §5)
- **Population:** Cohort regather. Each cohort survivor who finds Nate's ranch ticks the count toward the Stage 1→2 threshold
- **Likely Book 2 early.** The settlement progression *is* the cohort-reunion arc, mechanically
- **Threshold gating:** crossing the soul count alone doesn't activate Stage 2. Cornerstone resonance must also have grown into Tier 2 (TBD — see Open Decisions). Both required

### Stage 3 — Village (20+ souls)

- **Active metrics:** Full stat suite (Cornerstone resonance + Cohesion + Provision + Wellness + ?). Specific Stage 3 stat list deferred
- **Population:** Beyond cohort-only. Refugees, neighbors, allies from outside the original twelve
- **Book 2 late / Book 3.** Not a Book 1 concern

### Stage thresholds are firm gates, not soft

Crossing the population number without resonance tier does NOT activate the next stage's metrics. The numbers Flint surfaces stay limited to the current stage. This prevents the "stat suite expands automatically just because more people moved in" failure mode — and reinforces that the Cornerstone, not the headcount, is what holds the settlement together.

## 4. System-side parallel — one MSG Camera-pullaway

**Decision:** Book 1 includes exactly one Camera-pullaway scene showing a System-bound settlement counterpart. Specifications:

### Mechanical / aesthetic flavor — MSG-branded corporate town

- Morningstar Group logo glyph (M with star-tail) visible on signage, badges, building corners
- Residents framed as employees / contract-holders rather than as community members
- Branded blueprint catalogs, signing bonuses, intake forms
- Corporate-dystopia register, not pagan-temple register
- Boiling-frog principle: the scene reads *gentle, helpful, clean* on its surface. Reader recognizes the corruption only because they just watched Nate's mute, slow, no-instruction-manual arrival on the other side of the chapter
- Constraint: post-Tutorial only ([project_forge_path_choice_invariants](../../../Personal/CLAUDE.md) caps Tutorial at 0% MSG hooks, post-Tutorial at 20-40%)

### POV character — right-path secondary cohort member

- Candidate pool: **Heather Kim**, **Isabel Wu**, or **Kyle Greene** (right-path Tutorial graduates, secondary tier — not inner circle)
- Specific name pickable later when drafting
- Right-path self-selection is consistent with their established preference for easier shortcuts. MSG matches their disposition; landing there isn't character-assassination, it's character-tracking
- Inner-circle protection: this choice deliberately avoids Marcus / Josie / Ana so Book 2 reunion architecture stays open

### Placement — immediate post-scatter, same chapter as Nate-lands-alone

- Currently Ch10 per [forge_outline_restructure_2026-04-19](../../../plans/forge_outline_restructure_2026-04-19.md)
- Dual-scene structure within the Tutorial-ends chapter:
  - Nate at his fence line, alone, with Rex (locked from [ch05-restructure-summaries.txt](../../../plans/ch05-restructure-summaries.txt))
  - Camera pulls away to right-path cohort member arriving at MSG resettlement office
- Same beat — *being placed somewhere by the System / by the land* — rendered twice with opposite valences
- No exposition needed. The parallel does the work

## 5. Stat names — Stage 2

**Decision:** Stage 2 surfaces two metrics: **Cohesion** and **Provision**.

| Metric | What it tracks | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| **Cornerstone resonance** | Anchor's covenantal bond (always active) | Stage 1+ |
| **Cohesion** | Bond strength between souls and Cornerstone (relational-faculty index for the settlement) | Activates Stage 2 |
| **Provision** | Material supply state — food, gear, construction materials in flow | Activates Stage 2 |

### Naming principles

- **Mechanical-neutral, not religiously-flavored.** Same names work for clean-channel Conduit settlements and skewed-channel System settlements. The channel difference shows through behavior, not stat name. A System-bound town can have "Cohesion" too — theirs is just skewed-channel cohesion (orbiting around a Patron-anchored Throne rather than a Cornerstone)
- **Cornerstone resonance carries the spiritual weight.** Cohesion and Provision are descriptive material/social state. Keeping them secular prevents the trap The Land fell into with "Faith" — naming a metric religiously and thereby draining the religious vocabulary of meaning when it actually matters
- **Reader-comprehensible immediately.** No glossary required. Both words are self-explanatory in context

### Stage 3 stat suite — deferred

Working candidates: Cornerstone resonance + Cohesion + Provision + Wellness + Witness (or similar). Specific list deferred to Book 2 late / Book 3 design pass when village-scale gameplay actually surfaces.

## 7. System-side mechanics — additive parasitic layer

For when the System-side surfaces in Book 2+. Captured here as worldbuilding reference; not Book 1 content.

### Pay-to-win shortcuts

- **MSG paid blueprints** — pre-built structures available immediately for currency + a contract clause
- **Patron-favored buildings** — bonuses for accepting a Patron tag on the structure
- **Ascension tier paths** — settlement prestige classes with binding-depth costs

### Patron-anchored Throne

- The System-side Cornerstone counterpart
- A single building that anchors a Patron's foothold; settlement bonuses come from the bond
- Visible in MSG towns as the "executive tower" / corporate HQ; functionally a fallen-being's foothold dressed in office aesthetics
- Each level of Throne attunement = Codex #100 stage progression for residents

### Currency layer

- MSG scrip, contract tokens, branded loot
- Spendable; each transaction deepens System integration
- Alternative to the use-based growth path of the Conduit side

The boiling-frog architecture applies: no single MSG transaction reads as "selling your soul." Each one is a small convenience, a signing bonus, a reasonable contract. The cumulative effect is what locks the user in.
