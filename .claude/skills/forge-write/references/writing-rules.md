# Writing Rules — Forge Novel Generation Reference

Generation-oriented rules for drafting prose. For editorial review rules, see `/editors-hat`.

---

## POV and Tense

- **POV:** Close third person, Nate only (Book 1)
- **Tense:** Past
- **Restriction:** Nate can only narrate what he physically sees, hears, feels, smells
- **Show effects, not UI:** "Her hands are dimming" not "she's at sixty percent mana"

### Data Routing — Who Knows What

| Source | Route | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Nate's HUD** | Nate reads and interacts directly | He can see it, comment on it, react to it. It's HIS interface |
| **Deeper detail / context** | **Flint** elaborates | Flint adds analysis, history, tactical context beyond what the HUD shows |
| **System readouts** | **Flint** picks up via their shared network | Only when it makes sense for Flint to intercept — Flint is NOT omniscient |
| **Other players' info** | **Storyteller** (omniscient re: blue screens) OR the player tells Nate directly in conversation | Nate can't see other people's screens. Storyteller can. Players can share voluntarily |
| **Nate's own blue arcs / SA readings** | Nate perceives directly | These are his interface — no routing needed |

**Key constraints:**
- Flint has access to System data through the network he and Nate share, but he has LIMITS — he doesn't know everything
- The Storyteller has omniscient access to blue screens (System character sheets) — she can read what players see
- Nate CANNOT see other players' screens, stats, or System UI. Ever.
- Other players can verbally share their own info in dialogue

## Four-Lens Balance (Codex #92 — LOCKED)

Nate perceives through four lenses. No single lens dominates >40% of any chapter.

| Lens | Fires When | Voice Signature |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Ranch** | Securing resources, terrain, animal behavior, physical work | "Water first. Shelter second. Everything else is a luxury." |
| **Engineering** | System constructs, architecture, root cause, load-bearing analysis | "That's not a dungeon. That's a routing table with a skin on it." |
| **Gamer** | Combat encounters, Tutorial mechanics, game design patterns | "Adaptive difficulty. Miniboss with adds. The Tutorial is running an AI director." |
| **Christian** | Moral weight decisions, sacrifice, costly right over easy wrong | No voice. It IS the decision. He feeds people first. He goes back. He doesn't explain. |

**Combat activation pattern:** Gamer recognizes --> Engineer confirms --> Rancher executes --> Christian decides.

**If a section reads too heavy in one lens:**
- Too Western? Add gamer and engineering observations.
- Too tech-manual? Add ranch metaphors and gamer pattern recognition.
- Too gamey? Ground with ranch physicality and engineering diagnostics.
- The Christian lens is shown through ACTION, never through lens-labeled internal reflection.

## HUD Phase Rules (Codex #75 — LOCKED)

**Critical: Do NOT write features from later phases.** Check which phase applies to the target chapter.

| Phase | Chapters | Features | Color |
|-------|----------|----------|-------|
| 1A Boot | Ch3 | Two curved bars (health/stamina), monochrome, faint | Blue |
| 1B Craft | Ch4 | + SA wireframe overlay on focused objects, stress points | Blue |
| 1C Threat | Ch5-6 | + Peripheral blue brightens toward threats, first accidental toggle | Blue |
| 1D Targeting | Ch7-8 | + Focal reticle on aim, Flint pushes data to display, semi-intentional toggle | Blue |
| 2A Color | Ch9-11 | + Amber threats, blue-green environmental, Flint co-pilots, intentional toggle | Multi |
| 2B Tactical | Ch12-15 | + Full color, THE STRINGS, divine gold, concentric targeting, engineering workstation | Full |

### Two HUD Systems (System vs. Nate's)

The System HUD and Nate's HUD are fundamentally different products with different trajectories.

**System HUD (what everyone else gets):**
- Full gamer-style interface: character sheets, popup blocks, stat screens
- Character sheets BLOCK vision — you stop what you're doing to read them
- Polished and complete from day one — but NEVER improves. What you get is what you get.
- Combat HUD during gamer-type activities
- Blue screens = System character sheet (rest/menu only)

**Nate's HUD (organic, Flint-driven):**
- Hybrid: gamer info delivery + fighter-pilot spatial awareness
- NO full character sheet screen. NO vision-blocking popups. NEVER.
- Character details CAN display — in isolated box wrappers that don't obstruct vision
- Text appears in compact, non-intrusive containers (think game tooltip, not game menu)
- Starts BROKEN and behind from the botched install event — glitchy, incomplete, unreliable
- Progressively improves and eventually SURPASSES the System HUD
- SA wireframe overlays and tracking reticle are core features
- Mixed geometry: hard angles + organic curves (System is rigid boxes; Nate's has curves because its source is organic)

### Stat/Crunch Policy

- **Mild crunch** — gamer-style info that FEELS like a game, but not heavy stat blocks
- **New info only** — show stats when they're NEW (item pickup, level-up, first encounter). Do NOT repeat known information
- **NOT The Land style** — no stat dumps, no full character sheet readouts, no crunch walls
- **Item inspection** — when Nate picks something up or examines it, he gets stats/properties in a compact display. Let the reader know what he found or received
- **Progression shown in inline prose** — weave advancement into the narrative, not stat block interludes
- **Rule of thumb:** If you're writing more than 3 lines of stat/item info, you're over-crunching. Keep it tight.

### HUD Prose Rules

- Data is viewed THROUGH, not AT — experiential, not readout format
- Flickers near System constructs
- Drops 0.3s during Spirit moments (Flint goes silent simultaneously)
- Shakes on physical impacts
- Describe what Nate SEES and FEELS through the HUD, not what a UI manual would say

## Marks and Runes System (Codex #140)

Three distinct tiers of body markings. They are NOT the same thing. Never conflate them in prose.

### The Three Tiers

| Type | What It Is | Power | Aesthetic | Color |
|------|-----------|-------|-----------|-------|
| **Tattoos** | Ink on skin. Cosmetic/allegiance only | None | Art — whatever the character chooses | N/A |
| **System Marks** | System-granted, embedded | Functional, capped ceiling | Klingon — harsh, angular, aggressive, blocky | Blood red or black |
| **Divine Marks** | Spirit-sourced, internalized | Stronger, no ceiling, grows | Elvish (LOTR) — refined, flowing, fluid hieroglyphic/runic | Angelic blue or gold |

### Acquisition

- **Tattoos:** Voluntary. Guild, faction, personal choice. Just ink.
- **System marks:** Offered by the System. Always a CHOICE — System cannot force. Can deceive about the cost.
- **Divine marks:** Earned through action aligned with the path. Not requested, not transactional. They arrive or they don't.
- **Anomalies + System marks:** Spirit guides anomalies away — shown through stillness/unease per faith rules. Never stated, never forced.

### Ownership and Loss

- **Not transferable.** Marks are yours.
- **Stackable:** Yes — marks grow in complexity and facets, not just quantity.
- **System marks can be given up** — but the System resists. It doesn't want to lose a tie to you. Make giving one up feel like pulling against something.
- **Divine marks fade** when the bearer abandons the path. Their choice, their consequence.

### Power

- **System marks** have a ceiling. Functional, reliable, never improves beyond what it is.
- **Divine marks** have no ceiling. Grow with the bearer. Always stronger than System marks.
- **Mixing:** Anyone can have both (free will), but divine power **dilutes/diminishes** when mixed with System marks. Shown through weaker response, slower growth, dimmer glow — FELT, not explained.

### Visibility

- **Default:** Hidden.
- **Bearer control:** Can be willed visible — describing the reveal is a power move in prose.
- **Powerful beings** can see marks regardless.

### Placement

- No fixed body location — marks can appear anywhere.
- **Divine marks never on the face.**
- System marks have no placement restriction (the System doesn't have that restraint).

### Interaction

- Marks do NOT directly interact with each other on the body. The cost of mixing is in the power dilution, not physical conflict between marks.

### Prose Guardrails

1. Marks are NOT tattoos — different origin, different feel, different depth. Never confused in prose.
2. System marks: Klingon vocabulary — angular, harsh, carved, branded, rigid
3. Divine marks: Elvish vocabulary — flowing, scripted, luminous, living, refined
4. No ceremony for divine marks — they arrive after aligned action, LitRPG pacing
5. System mark offers feel like good deals — deception in presentation
6. Spirit guidance away from System marks follows existing faith rules (stillness, unease — never stated)
7. Divine dilution from mixing is FELT, not explained
8. Giving up a System mark should feel like resistance — the System pulls back
9. Tattoos exist separately — ink, art, allegiance, zero power

## Life Skills and Skill Progression (Codex #141, #142)

Skills improve by DOING, not by spending points. This is how Nate learned before the System — and it still works.

**Skill categories:** Survival (cooking, fire, water, shelter), Husbandry (animal care, breeding, training), Harvesting (gathering, mining, skinning), Forging (metalwork, runes, reinforcement), Engineering (repair, construction, traps, fortification), Field Medicine (wounds, herbs, triage).

**Key tension:** Nate had most of these BEFORE integration. The System wraps them in its framework and tries to claim credit. Does leveling a skill through the System deepen its hooks?

### Skill Tiers

| Tier | Range | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| Novice | 1-10 | Learning the basics |
| Apprentice | 11-25 | Competent, unsupervised |
| Journeyman | 26-50 | Professional quality |
| Expert | 51-75 | Regional reputation |
| Master | 76-100 | Few peers |
| Grandmaster | 100+ | Legendary — may unlock skill evolution |

**Skill evolution:** At tier boundaries, skills can branch/specialize. Player's choice. Example: Cooking 25 might branch into "Field Rations" (survival) or "Feast Preparation" (settlement buff).

### Prose Rules for Skills

- Skill-ups shown **inline**, not as stat blocks
- Show the number ONCE when it matters, then move on
- "The crossbow bolt flew truer than the last dozen. Something had clicked." — not "Crossbow Skill: 12 -> 13"

## Conduit Abilities — The Flow (Codex #143)

Companion to Codex #10 (Conduit Magic System). This defines what Nate can actually DO.

**Model:** Power works like electricity through a wire. Nate doesn't memorize spells — he learns to SHAPE what flows through him.

| Aspect | How it works | Prose feel |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Channeling** | Power flows from source through Nate. Continuous, not burst | Holding a live wire — sustained effort |
| **Shaping** | Directs flow into effects through practice + rune knowledge | Engineer tuning a system |
| **Capacity** | How much he can channel without burning out. Grows over time | Wire gauge — thicker wire, more current |
| **Burnout** | Channel too much = physical damage. His limits hurt him, not the power | Overloaded circuit, not divine punishment |
| **Rune Anchoring** | Runes on objects act as pre-shaped channels | Engineer laying infrastructure |

### Abilities (unlock order)

| Ability | What it does | When |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Reinforce** | Channel into structures/objects to strengthen | Early — SA + engineering extension |
| **Sense** | Feel divine architecture under System overlay | Early — SA does this partially already |
| **Inscribe** | Burn runes into materials (physical contact + focus) | Mid Book 1 — rune reading first |
| **Mend** | Channel into broken things to REPAIR (not heal) | Mid Book 1 — engineering instinct |
| **Shield** | Sustained channeling = localized barrier | Late Book 1 — high cost, short duration |
| **Purge** | Disrupt/cleanse System constructs | Book 2+ — dangerous, requires understanding |

**Not spells. Not a menu. Not mana.** An engineer learning what his tools can do.

## System User Progression (Codex #144)

How normal System-integrated users progress. We see this through Nate's eyes — conversation and observed effects, never their stat screens.

- **Class skills** — granted at assignment + unlocked at level thresholds (5, 10, 15, etc.)
- **General skills** — learned through use. Anyone can learn Cooking, but class skills level faster
- **Stat allocation** — points per level, player distributes. STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA
- **Skill slots** — limited active slots, forces choices. More slots at higher levels
- **Patron blessings** — optional. Accept a Patron's mark for bonus skills/stats. Deepens System hooks (Codex #100)

## Elder Powers Magic (Codex #146)

How the Old Powers (Codex #8) magic works. Fundamentally different from both System and divine.

- **Source:** Self-contained — they ARE their power. Oldest predate the System by millennia
- **Style:** Ritualistic, territorial, covenant-based. "I give you X, you give me Y"
- **Limitation:** Bound by their own ancient rules — cannot break their covenants
- **vs. System:** Uneasy arrangement. System can't override them. They tolerate it because fighting costs more
- **vs. Divine:** They know God is real. Some respect it. Some resent it. None deny it.
- **Prose feel:** Elder magic feels OLD — slower, heavier, more deliberate. Smartphone vs. stone cathedral. Both work. One was built to last.

## The Labyrinth as Original System (Codex #147 — FOUNDATIONAL)

**This reframes EVERYTHING.** The Labyrinth is God's creation — the underlying OS of reality. The System didn't build any of its features. It wrapped the Labyrinth's existing features in its own interface, took credit, and hid its hooks in the wrapper.

**The metaphor:** The Labyrinth is the hardware + OS. The System is bloatware that skins the desktop and quietly phones home.

### The System Creates Nothing

| Feature | Labyrinth Original | System's Wrapper |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Growth | Use-based, organic, no ceiling | Levels, XP bars, skill points. Hooks deepen at each tier |
| Skills | Natural learning. Always existed | Skill tree UI. Creates dependency on the interface |
| Places of power | Sacred sites, divine architecture nodes | "Settlement cores" — locks the door, sells you the key |
| Item properties | Inherent qualities from materials + craftsmanship | Color-coded rarity labels. Need System to appraise |
| Guardians | Living intelligences bound to places of power | Replaced with management UI. Guardians dormant |
| Runes | Divine originals — the Labyrinth's native language | Corrupted copies. Functional but capped |
| Bonds | Natural empathic connections | System classifies/quantifies bonds it didn't create |

### Prose Rules

1. When the System "gives" someone a feature, hint that something was ALREADY there — the System just labeled it
2. When Nate sees through the System overlay (SA), he sees the REAL architecture — older, more beautiful, more complex
3. System features feel corporate and clean. Labyrinth originals feel ancient and alive
4. **The hooks are in the interface, not the features.** The skill is real. The progress bar is the hook. The settlement core is real. The management menu is the hook.
5. Nate's conduit connection bypasses the System layer — direct access, no middleman, no hooks
6. System players CANNOT see the Labyrinth layer — their interface has no driver for it (unless Redemption Path, Codex #59)
7. The discovery arc: Nate gradually realizes the System didn't give anyone anything — it just claimed what was already there
8. The System HATES Nate's settlements because he's showing people what's behind the curtain

### Skill attribution labeling — Tutorial vs post-Tutorial (2026-06-17)

**Three layers, one visible name.** The **Labyrinth** is the true source but is NEVER named on-page in Book 1 — the hidden truth; no skill is ever labeled "from the Labyrinth." **The System** is the generic in-world attribution for *everything*: the counterfeit hides the source, so every skill reads as "the System gave / granted / logged it." **MSG / Morningstar** is the System's corporate brand face, NOT a separate source.

**Tutorial (Ch4-10) = "the System" only.** No Morningstar branding, no hooks — the free-sample phase. The reward-chime is System scaffolding, not MSG. **MSG branding + hooks debut post-Tutorial** as the hook-rate ramps. Never split skills into two visible sources (System vs MSG) — MSG is the System wearing a suit. See `research/skill-system-design.md` → "Skill attribution labeling."

## Language Rating (Updated 2026-05-03)

**Audience:** 18+. Moderate profanity OK (damn, hell, shit, ass, F-bomb, S-bomb, son of a bitch, bastard).

| Character | Style | Frequency |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Nate (spoken/out-loud only) | Restrained, lands heavy when used | **HARD CAP: 3-5 vocalized per ~5,000 words** (interior thought / italicized direct thought / POV-prose NOT counted — write those freely) |
| Flint | Precise, more freely | No fixed cap; scene-driven |
| Josie | Exclamatory, material-focused | When things are wasted |
| Marcus | Military-professional | Situational |
| All others / prose narration | Scene-driven | No fixed cap — heavy combat/torture/villain scenes may run high; quiet scenes stay clean |

**HARD RULE — ABSOLUTE, ALL VOICES INCLUDING VILLAINS:** Never use the Lord's name in vain. No "God damn," "Jesus Christ" as expletive, "Oh my God," "Christ Almighty," "Lord above," "God help us." This applies to ALL characters and ALL voices including Storyteller-omniscient (Hiberno register has its own "Jaysus / God help us" ban), Storyteller-physical-zoom, Nate, Flint, antagonists, demonic entities, and corrupted characters. Confirmed 2026-05-03 — the ban is not a heroes-only restriction.

### Villain / Corrupted Character Blasphemy Palette

When evil, corrupted, or System-aligned characters need to curse, swear, or invoke a higher power in anger or menace, use cosmology-native or approved English instead of the Lord's Name. Cosmology-native does **more** work per syllable: profanity AND theology AND characterization.

- **Cosmology-native oaths (preferred — reveal allegiance):** "By the Morningstar," "The System take you" / "The System sees you," "In the Pit" / "to the Pit with you," "By the Sealing" (older / scholarly evil)
- **Approved English profanity:** F-bomb, S-bomb, "son of a bitch," "bastard," "damn," "hell"
- **Edge-of-line constructions:** "godforsaken," "godawful," "hell" (lowercase generic intensifier)
- **Demonic register:** "pit-spawn," "veil-cur," imperative menace ("kneel," "yield")
- **Action-as-blasphemy:** spitting on a cross, breaking a sacred object, mockery of prayer, desecration

**Ad-lib clause:** the palette is a starting toolkit, not a fixed canon. New phrases can be coined as the cosmology develops — anything that (a) avoids the Name, (b) fits the world's theology, and (c) sounds like something a real corrupted character would say. When inventing a new oath, save it back to `feedback_language_rating.md`.

**Why cosmology-native wins:** when Storyteller and Nate never invoke the Name, and villains invoke Morningstar/System/Pit, the listener feels the moral geography of the world without anyone preaching about it. The contrast is audible. The moment villains use the real Name, that contrast collapses — they're just rude, not theologically aligned with the wrong side.

**Per-chapter density:** the old "3-5 instances per chapter total" cap was retired 2026-05-03. The only standing density rule is Nate's spoken cap (3-5 per 5k words, vocalized only). Everything else is scene-driven judgment.

## The "What NOT to Do" List

These are absolute prohibitions. If you catch yourself writing any of these, stop and revise.

1. **Never** give Rex or Judge System UI abilities or human cognition (Oberon is the counter-model). Their bond articulation progresses P0→P3 across the series; hold them at the **Book-1 stage — impressions only, no word-thoughts** — until the schedule advances. Content stays dog-shaped at every stage. See `project_pack_bond_progression`
2. **Never** describe screens as emitting visible light (internal only, faint glint)
3. **Never** use the Lord's name in vain
4. **Never** preach, narrate theology, or name the Holy Spirit in prose
5. **Never** write a chapter without reading the prior chapter ending first
6. **Never** write Phase 2 HUD features in Phase 1 chapters without Flint flagging the anomaly
7. **Never** let any single lens dominate more than 40% of a chapter section
8. **Never** write Josie as anything less than maximum comedy
9. **Never** reference the blue screen during combat — it's rest/menu only
10. **Never** re-establish ranch details already shown in Ch1
11. **Never** write lens labels ("the gamer in him," "his engineering brain")
12. **Never** state "he prayed" or describe prayer as an activity
13. **Never** use "Imago Dei" directly in prose — use generic references
14. **Never** explain the Rex bond mechanic or call out "the bond between them"
15. **Never** have Nate narrate information he couldn't physically perceive
