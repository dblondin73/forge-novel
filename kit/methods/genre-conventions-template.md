# Genre Conventions & Reader Contract — Method Template (Standards)

A portable method for capturing the **reader-expectation contract** of a genre as a
*checkable reference*, distinct from canon (the *what*) and craft rules (the *how*).
Fill this template with your genre and stances; the project's filled copy becomes a
Novel-layer doc (in this repo: `LITRPG_CONVENTIONS.md`).

The method is the Standard; the content is the Novel's.

---

## The three stances

Every convention the genre's reader expects gets one stance:

- **HONOR** — meet the expectation straight. The reader gets what they came for.
- **BEND** — deliver the underlying *payoff* by your own means. The reader's
  *need* is met; the *form* differs.
- **BREAK** — deliberately refuse the expectation. A break must be intentional and
  earn its place. **An accidental break is a bug; a deliberate one is the work's
  signature.**

The operational test, every time a beat leans on a convention: *are we honoring,
bending, or breaking it — and is the stance the one the doc records?* A stance the
doc does **not** record is a finding.

---

## Authority — this layer yields

A genre convention never overrides canon, craft, or the project's master
tiebreaker. The doc records what the reader *expects*, so divergence is a *choice*,
not an accident. Place it **below** canon and craft in the authority order.

---

## Template structure (fill per project)

1. **Header** — what this doc is (reader-contract), what it is *not* (canon /
   craft), and the source it's distilled from (your influence/reference bank —
   patterns only, no reproduced text).
2. **Authority note** — the yield order.
3. **Stance legend** — HONOR / BEND / BREAK as above.
4. **Convention groups** (one table each; keep groups small and scannable). Each
   row: *convention | what the reader expects | stance | how this work does it.*
   Typical groups: interface/UI, progression/payoff, onboarding logic, stakes,
   cast/social furniture, pacing/serialization, genre furniture.
5. **Signature divergences** — gather the big deliberate BREAKs/BENDs into one
   "don't re-litigate" list so they're never mistaken for bugs on review.
6. **Editor's drift scan** — a short list of *unintended*-break flags the editor
   greps for (the stances that, if they appear unrecorded, are findings).
7. **Writer's consult** — a one-paragraph instruction: before drafting a beat that
   leans on a convention, check the stance; deliver the payoff even when the form
   bends; never honor a convention the work deliberately breaks.
8. **Portability note** — this file is the swappable genre layer.

---

## Why it's checkable, not decorative

The value is the *stance column* tied to the work's actual canon, plus the
drift-scan. Without stances it's a generic listicle; with them, an editor pass can
ask "is this honor/bend/break, and is it the recorded one?" and a writer can avoid
honoring a convention the work has chosen to break.
