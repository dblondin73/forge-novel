## Core Characters

**Nathan "Nate" Hall** — Codex #1
Mid-30s Louisiana cattleman turned Engineer and DoD contractor. 6'2" to 6'3", stocky, farm fit — not bodybuilder bulk. Decades of ranch work. System class: Anomaly — Conduit of the Most High. Channels divine power (conduit, not battery); power flows when aligned with God's purpose, stops when it isn't. Hidden bloodline traces to King David (#60). Three name modes: "Nathan" (formal), "Nate" (ranch/friends), "King" (earned leadership title). Interface is a fighter-pilot HUD, not the standard blue screen. **Four-lens narrator** (#92): Ranch, Engineering, Gamer, Christian — no single lens dominates more than 40% of any chapter. Casual gamer background: Diablo, Elder Scrolls, casual WoW, Destiny, XCOM/Civ, some tabletop D&D in college. Hunting and fishing since childhood — rifles, shotguns, deer stands. Pre-System class: **Engineer** (#98). Tutorial loadout: improvised crossbow + reinforced hammer + Thornling thorn bolts (#104).

**F.L.I.N.T.** — Codex #2
Field Logic Interface, Network Tethered. Nate's AI companion — a standard System construct rewritten by the Holy Spirit before first boot. Sarcastic, sharp-tongued, loyal. James Marsters / Bob the Skull energy. Outbound reporting permanently severed (no spyware); inbound network access intact but degraded via ghost authentication token. Mental link with Nate is two-way from first contact. Network capability progresses across all 7 books from fragmented receive-only (Book 1) to root write access at the Keystone (Book 7). Consent rule locked from Book 4 forward.

**Sonja Lee** — Codex #3
Red-haired, green-eyed Anomaly of Irish descent. Very fit — athletic, strong, warrior's frame (Red Sonja parallel intentional). Beautiful enough that people notice — a liability when the System is hunting her. Name from Greek Sophia — she IS wisdom in this partnership. Spiritual gift: **The Witness** (#73) — sees the true nature of what she looks at. Cannot be deceived. Celtic heritage gives thin-place resonance. Romance develops slowly across books. Challenges Nate the way Nathan the prophet challenged King David.

**Rex** — Codex #64
Nate's Border Collie. Four years old at Integration. Working stock dog, not a pet. Communicates through behavior and empathic impressions — never words, never speech (#72). Survives Integration, transported to Tutorial via companion bond (#88). Post-Integration evolution through **Companion Resonance**: heightened senses, empathic amplification, physical edge, pack tactics. Resonance is conduit-based, not System-granted.

**Marcus Webb** — Codex #86
Nate's boss at the DoD contracting firm. The Adapter — recognizes the new reality faster than most. Tutorial class: **Vanguard** (#99). In Nate's Tutorial pocket by geography. Manages the pocket's defense the way he managed programs.

**Josie Pickett — The Loot Goblin** — Codex #102
Early 20s, East Texas estate sale junkie / pawn shop worker. System class: **Appraiser** (#103) — identifies items and materials. System-integrated (NOT an Anomaly). Compulsively collects every drop and scrap. "Are you just going to LEAVE that there?" Primary comic relief alongside Flint — manic earnest enthusiasm vs. precise dry sarcasm. Supplies Thornling thorn bolts for Nate's crossbow. Long-term: settlement quartermaster/scavenger. Potential Redemption Path convert.

**The Stag** — Codex #78
Mythological stag that appears on Nate's ranch at the moment of Integration. NOT a whitetail — wrong in every proportion. Coat holds light warmer than it receives. Eyes gold/amber, self-luminous. The stag's eye contact with Nate is the trigger. Part of God's infrastructure. Identity TBD — hybrid Welsh/Celtic Christian/Norse direction. Reappears.

**Thornlings** — Codex #90
Tutorial-spawned creatures — first enemies. Small (3-4 feet), fast, pack-hunting. Bark-textured skin, thorn-studded limbs, Fae aesthetic. Bleed amber sap. Scalable: Thornlings (L1-3), Thornguards (L5-8, bark-plate armor), Briarknights (L10+, boss tier). The overlay produces counterfeit life from counterfeit environment.

**Briarknight — Tutorial Boss** — Codex #106
Eight feet tall. Interlocking armor plates — layered like dragon scale but grown from wood. The Tutorial took everything it learned from nine rooms and built something custom. Weapon: massive glaive-like arm growth, monocrystalline thorn blade. Three dangers: smart (targets Healer first), armored beyond F-to-E rank weapons, adaptive (learns from combat data). The pinnacle of what the counterfeit can produce — impressive but still derivative.

**The Stage 4 Convert** — Codex #101
Identity TBD. Books 5-6. Fully hollowed System construct who chooses the door. Proves grace never expires. Devastating cost — emerges as nearly nothing. One character, one time, prevents binding progression from reading as theological determinism.

## Factions

**The System** — Codex #4
Lucifer's counterfeit management layer installed on God's creation. Presents as empowerment. Functions as control — quantifies identity, replaces relationship with the Creator.

**The Creator** — Codex #5
The one true God — Father, Son, Holy Spirit. Authority supersedes everything. Does not override free will. Not a faction — the foundational reality.

**The Anomalies** — Codex #6
True believers whose integration failed because the Holy Spirit already occupies the space. Thousands worldwide (1 Kings 19:18, scaled to 8 billion), exact count deliberately uncounted. Recognize each other by "a knowing."

**The Patrons — Fallen Beings in System Service** — Codex #7
Fallen spiritual beings who accepted Lucifer's centralized authority in exchange for territory and worshippers. Patron bonds are the System's counterfeit of prayer.

**The Old Powers — Global Mythological Beings** — Codex #8
Powerful spiritual beings from every tradition. All real created beings — none are gods. Predate the System. Response not uniform: some joined, some resisted, some retreated.

**The Faithful Watchers** — Codex #9
Spiritual beings who remained loyal to the Creator. Provide guidance and occasional open doors. May have hidden pre-System artifacts in sacred sites.

## Power Systems (Locked — Session 013)

**The Barrier — Imago Dei Foundation** — Codex #94
Humans were always superhuman — created in God's image, that power was natural in Eden. After the Fall, a barrier was placed over it. The System artificially bypasses this barrier and claims credit for what God built. For Anomalies, the Holy Spirit ACTUALLY removes the barrier but without training wheels.

**Power Hierarchy — Three Tiers** — Codex #91
Imago Dei = foundation baked into everything, not a separate tier. Tier 1: System Magic (capped at **A-tier** in absolute terms). Tier 2: Elder Magic — Fae/Old Powers, the fragment, degraded photocopy. Tier 3: Conduit Power (uncapped, conditional on faithfulness). God acts ONLY through the conduit, never directly. No Tier 4.

**System Magic Rules — Four Components** — Codex #97
Light crunch — readers see costs/cooldowns, no formulas. (1) Mana pools + cooldowns. (2) Skill ranks F→S (use-based leveling, addictive dopamine loop). (3) Class affinities (hybrid assignment — aptitude + System need, illusion of choice). (4) Patron bonds (power shortcuts with hidden binding cost). Universal starter skill for all classes.

**Conduit Consequence Model** — Codex #93
Two modes: Minor drift (pride, selfishness) → gradual power fade like a weakening signal. Major violation (deliberate cruelty, using power for evil) → hard shutoff, breaker flips. Restoration = relationship repair, not cooldown timer.

**System Binding Progression — Four Stages** — Codex #100
The mirror of conduit consequences. Stage 1 Integration (The Gift — free, invisible hooks). Stage 2 Leveling (The Hooks — dependency grows). Stage 3 Patron Bonds (The Chains — autonomy erodes). Stage 4 Prestige/Ascension (The Replacement — humanity replaced). The door is ALWAYS open but some become someone who would never walk through it. The Boiling Frog Principle.

**System Hooks vs System Tools** — Codex #108
Not everything the System provides is a hook. HOOKS (bind deeper): skills, class advancement, patron bonds, AI spyware, prestige paths. TOOLS (functional, no strings): standard weapons/armor, inventory rings, consumables, basic crafting. A dishonest system that makes everything a trap would be too obvious — honest tools build trust, trust makes hooks invisible.

## Interface & HUD

**HUD Interface System** — Codex #11
Fighter-pilot HUD, not blue screen. During Integration, Nate briefly receives the standard blue interface (5 sec) before it degrades to two monochrome green arcs (Phase 1 Basic). All screens internal only. Four phases: Basic → Tactical → Analytical → Architectural.

**HUD Visual Design and Prose Format** — Codex #75
Mixed geometry: hard angles (triangles, targeting shapes) combined with rounded elements (arcs, compass-rose circles). System UI is rigid boxes; Nate's HUD has organic curves because its source is organic. Phase 1: monochrome green, health/stamina only. Phase 2 (Ch25+): green base, amber threats, blue-green environmental, gold divine architecture. Phase 3 (Books 3-4): structural analysis overlay, dense data panels. Phase 4 (Books 5-7): full dual-layer vision, System + divine simultaneously. Prose rule: HUD data described experientially, not as readout.

**System Dual Interface** — Codex #95
Blue screen redesigned as **character sheet only** — a rest/menu screen that blocks vision when open (like Skyrim inventory). System users also get a separate **combat HUD** for active use. Starts better than Nate's but never evolves. Nate has NO character sheet — his info is felt and interacted with mentally through his HUD.

**Screen Visibility Rules** — Codex #80
All System interfaces are internal only — no physical light emitted. The only external tell is a faint blue glint behind the eyes.

## Game Mechanics — Magic & Progression

**Conduit Magic System** — Codex #10
Nate channels power from God, doesn't store it. Output variable — exceeds maximum when aligned, zero when selfish.

**Spiritual Progression Track** — Codex #12
Hidden progression the System can't track. Intuition → Discernment → Active Guidance → Authority.

**WIS as Primary Stat** — Codex #26
WIS 20 is Nate's highest stat. Drives Perception, Discernment, resistance to System manipulation. Bridges System track and spiritual track.

Magic exists. The distinction is source. System magic = Lucifer's counterfeit. Conduit magic = from God.

**Counterfeit Evolution** — Codex #15

**Redemption Path — System De-Integration** — Codex #59
Party mechanic. Characters can be freed from System binding. Devastating cost. Core series-wide theology.

**Integration Memory Loss** — Codex #79
System scan converts memories sector by sector. For Anomalies, edge memories lost, core identity protected.

## Tutorial (Sessions 013-014 — Major Rewrite)

**Tutorial Room Structure** — Codex #96
50 people per pocket (down from 200). Room-based dungeon run — 11 rooms (Room 0 staging through Room 10 boss). Safe room start with fantasy starter gear (crap quality). One fight per room. Rest corridors with crafting stations (basic table → forge → workshop). Loot drops. Varied environments. Group split at Room 6. Moral test: Resource Split (12 gear sets, 25 people — Nate gives his away). Boss room: Briarknight + adds. Nate exits Level 4 Engineer.

**Tutorial Glitch Abilities** — Codex #87
System attempts standard starter abilities, but Nate's occupied space warps what comes through. SA shows actual architecture instead of item stats. Combat skill fails → Field Repair activates. Post driver does NOT come to Tutorial. Nate builds weapons using SA + Field Repair at crafting stations.

**Room 10 Boss Fight — Three Phases** — Codex #107
Phase 1 (Adds): Thornling swarm, Briarknight watches, drains group to ~60%. Phase 2 (The Knight): Briarknight advances, targets Healer, adapts in real-time, Shield Guardian goes down. Phase 3 (Engineer's Answer): Nate sees structural weakness via SA — arena pillar is load-bearing, armor is brittle under compression. Environmental kill: pillar collapse pins the Briarknight. Team finishes it — Warriors into the gaps. **The Engineer doesn't kill the boss. The Engineer makes the boss killable.** Post-boss: loot chest, Josie harvests arm-blade, graduation, Level 4 exit, back to Earth.

**Tutorial Pet Bond and Companion Resonance** — Codex #88
Animals with strong pre-existing bonds transported alongside owner. For Anomaly-bonded companions, enhancement is stronger. NOT a System feature.

**Tutorial Child Protection** — Codex #83
Children vanish to secure holding zone before adults are transported. Instant and terrifying.
